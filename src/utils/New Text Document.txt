Bug 1 — "once" lane contamination + line break glitch
convertEnglishNumberWords now takes an optional lang parameter ('en' | 'es', default 'en'). Each lane gets its own word map: EN_NUMBER_MAP and ES_NUMBER_MAP. When the English pane calls convertEnglishNumberWords(text, 'en'), Spanish words like "once" are invisible to the regex. When the Spanish pane calls convertEnglishNumberWords(text, 'es'), English words like "one" or "two" are invisible. The line break bug was caused by the replacement swallowing trailing punctuation — fixed by capturing an optional [,.]? group after the word and re-emitting it.
Bug 2 — SSN formatted as phone
formatPhoneAndSSNDigits now branches on digitsOnly.length === 9 first and formats it as NNN-NN-NNNN (SSN) rather than falling through to the generic dash-grouping logic that was producing phone-style output.
New export — detectSentinelContext(text, lang)
Returns { mode: 'spelling' | 'email' | 'ssn' | 'phone' | 'address' | null, lang }. Call it in your STT pipeline on the raw transcript chunk before number conversion or digit formatting. If mode is non-null, you can suppress or soften the downstream transforms — for example, skipping convertEnglishNumberWords entirely during a spelling run, or skipping formatPhoneAndSSNDigits when the patient just said "my mailing address is". Bilingual sentinels exist for both 'en' and 'es'.
New export — looksLikeAddressFragment(textBefore, textAfter)
Returns true if the surrounding context contains directional words (North, West, etc.) or street-type words (Street, Avenue, Blvd, etc.). You can call this inside your digit-joining logic to skip reformatting when a digit run is clearly a street number like "34" in "34th Street".
What you need to do in the call sites

Pass lang: 'en' or lang: 'es' to convertEnglishNumberWords based on which pane is being processed.
Optionally wire detectSentinelContext at the top of your STT onTranscript handler and gate the other transforms behind it.
looksLikeAddressFragment is a helper you can call inside your digit-processing logic in useDeepgram.js — pass the text before and after the digit cluster.

All previously exported names and their signatures are unchanged, so no other call sites break.
